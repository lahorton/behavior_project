( Behavior Interventions)

  1. Choose a behavior you'd like to address and thoughtfully select ONE intervention to address it.

  2. Collect baseline data. Collect 3-5 data-points before starting the intervention to determine the studentâ€™s baseline, or starting point, in the behavior that is being addressed. This baseline will be useful to compare to the the outcome and measure progress after implementing the intervention.

  3. Determine the length of the intervention. Good practice suggests 6 weeks, or at least 6 data points, to evaluate meaningful progress.

  4. Based on the baseline and the length of the intervention, determine the intervention goal.

  5. Once you've tried the intervention for the determined timeframe, evaluate progress. This is a good opportunity to reflect on how appropriate the intervention was for the targetted behavior, strategize next steps, and celebrate student success!
